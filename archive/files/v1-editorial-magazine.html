Cover Story · Page 04

The portfolio of a builder, not a deck-maker.

Most product managers ship slides. The candidate ships infrastructure — voice, chat, SMS, email, mobile, all unified into one agent autonomously handling billing, payments, and outage support across every resident channel. This is the file.

The pattern in Shivam Sharma's career resists the usual product-management résumé arithmetic, which prefers titles, headcount, and stage. The actual signal is this: at 19 he founded a marketing agency that grew to serve two hundred clients across the United Kingdom and the Middle East. At 22 he co-founded a real-estate CRM automation business that managed a two-hundred-thousand-dollar marketing budget and influenced more than five million dollars in closed deals. By 24, both ventures had been acquired — combined exit five hundred and seventy-five thousand dollars, ninety-five percent retention post-acquisition, no outside capital ever taken.

What followed was the kind of career-collapse most founders dread and few execute deliberately: a master's program in AI in Business at Simon, completed in eighteen months, STEM-certified. A founding-PM stint at Vollie shipping a voice-AI coaching platform from zero to one as the sole product owner. A product-lead role at the Ain Center where he replaced a twenty-five-thousand-dollar-per-year licensed simulator with VentureForge, an AI strategy sim grading fifty MBA decisions against real fuel prices and market shocks.

Then ESC Partners. The role title reads AI Product Manager, but the job description reads engineer. The deliverable is Maya AI: a full operating layer on Oracle's Customer Care and Service product, built in two and a half months by a single person, currently live for two hundred thousand residents at the New England Power utility, rolling out at Moreno Valley Electrical Utility, piloting at Delta Utilities, with a supervisor cockpit that runs sentiment analysis on twenty-six-language voice traffic via 11Labs and one-click human takeover. Seventy to eighty percent of first-touch volume, automated. One hundred manager emails per day, eliminated. Two derivatives spun off: a standalone email triage agent at NEP, and VaultIQ — an air-gapped Claude Opus platform deployed inside the customer's VPC on Next.js, Postgres with pgvector, and Terraform with single sign-on.
